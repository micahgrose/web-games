BASE OF THE GAME:



For each persons first turn, the AI should ask them who they want to be. They cannot do any actions, they are just setting up who they are.

Whenever somebody makes an action AGAINST ANOTHER PLAYER (or more) than before anything happens, the affected players are given the chance to say what they want to do about it or how they want to counter. Then cards are drawn for every affected player and for the player affecting them. If an affected player's card is higher than the player's card who is affecting them, then it is countered FOR THAT PLAYER ONLY. example: player 1: I want to push player 2 and player 3 off a cliff. (Player 2 and player 3's text prompts are unlocked). Player 2: I want to dodge around player 1. Player 3: I want to grab player 1's hands and pull him off the cliff. (cards are drawn for player 1, 2 and 3. Player 1 gets a value of 9. Player 2 gets a value of 5. Player 3 gets a value of 10.) AI: As player 1 starts pushing player 3 off the cliff, player 3 grabs player 1's hands and throws him off instead. Player 2 attempts to dodge around player 1, but gets knocked off with him, both falling to the ground and dying. (or somethign like that).
Make sure that all cards are drawn in the same animation, and that the player's usernames are over their drawn card. insure that the submit button for the affected players is enabled.

Make sure that the AI takes into account previous actions and happenings better. For example, if player 1 gets wounded on someones turn, then on his turn, unless he is doing something to fix his wounds, then he needs much higher cards to carry out actions. or if player 2 has really powerful armor, then player 3 needs a higher then usual card to penetrate it. Or if player 4 has really strong telekinesis powers and tries to throw player 5 into deep space, He can succeed with a slightly lower card than player 5.

Make sure that player's in the same game cannot have the same name or be the same thing. They can also not use the exact same prompt twice, however they can attempt the same thing from a different angle.

Use the AI to govern wether players are going to be affected or not by an action, wether a player needs slightly higher or slightly lower cards than usual, and if a player is just repeating the same thing over and over. Make sure that if a player submits a completly jibberish prompt or a prompt that is not understood by an english person, that the AI can give the user a new turn to give a prompt it can make sense of. Wierd and insane fantasy can happen, but plain jibberish is not ok. Example for ok: I want to be a purple dragon who can build a really cool wall and name it bob and make it come to life. I want to give the wall the power to turn a person to tuingsten. Example for not ok: a;owkjb;lasdjfi ;wajfadkjdkck. Or: Boiefkk. As long as there is a small bit of detail and it is understandable in english, the prompt is ok.
