about this chapter

Chapter 4 — the real thing

What this is: a genuine miniature GPT. It runs multi-head self-attention at every position, stacked in layers — the same architecture behind today's chatbots, shrunk to a tab.

Step up from TinyChat: its attention was a simplification (one head, last word only). This is the full recipe, so it reaches coherent replies in far fewer steps, with crisper top-k/top-p sampling.

Experiment freely in the 🎮 sandbox, or break a belief on the myth pages (the next steps).

This demo's settings: memory 24 · neurons 48 · heads 2 · layers 2 · data 400.
Why these? the balanced "fastest-to-coherent" setup — enough capacity and depth to learn the task, small enough to train in a minute or two. (Change them in free play.)

Heads & layers

🔦 Heads

A head is one attention "spotlight" — it decides which earlier words matter. Each head specializes (topic, question-ness, tone). 1 head = one thread at a time (≈ TinyChat); 2–4 track several at once.

🧱 Layers

A layer is one stage of refinement: words → phrases → intent, built up in steps. 1 is shallow; 2–3 compose real meaning, at more math and more training.

Feel the difference on the depth myth page, or compare heads/layers in free play.
