about this chapter

Chapter 3 — a network learns to converse

What this is: a small-talk bot, and your first transformer. It reads whole words, gets a first taste of attention (looking back at earlier words), and holds a short multi-turn chat with phases.

Step up from TinyTalk: words carry more meaning than letters; attention lets it refer back; phases teach it to greet, chat and sign off differently.

An honest catch: the attention is simplified (one head, last word only). Chapter 4 (TinyGPT) builds the real thing.

This demo's settings: memory 16 · neurons 48 · data 120.
Why these? small context fits short turns, modest width avoids overfitting, medium data gives enough variety to generalize. (Change them in free play.)
