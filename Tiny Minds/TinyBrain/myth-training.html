Myth: "more training always makes it better"

"Just let it train longer and it keeps improving."

✗ The myth: more steps always means a better model.
✓ The truth: every network has a ceiling. Once it has learned what it can, more steps do nothing — the limit is the network's size, not the amount of training.
👀 Watch: press Train and leave it running as long as you like. The right square improves fast, then stalls well short and never closes the gap — no matter how many epochs pass. A network this small simply can't represent a spiral, so more training does nothing.
🔁 For contrast, the free-play sandbox lets you point this same network at Gradient — and it nails it almost perfectly. Same training, different ceiling. Time can't buy what the network can't represent.
This page stays on the Spiral — the one pattern too complex for this tiny network to represent. There's deliberately nothing to pause or swap here: the whole point is that you can train it forever and it still won't close the gap. (Other patterns live in free play.)
