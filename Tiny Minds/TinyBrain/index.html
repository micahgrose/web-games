TinyBrain

Chapter 1 — a network learns to see

What this is: a real neural network (2 neurons — the "knobs" — → 16 → 16 → colour), built from scratch. Its only job: look at a pixel's position and decide what colour it should be. The left picture is the goal; the right is what the network has learned so far.

Why it matters: this is learning in its purest form. The network starts random (right square = mess), and each training step nudges its knobs so its picture looks a little more like the truth. The accuracy climbing is you literally watching it learn.

This page walks through one pattern so you can watch it succeed. Try the rest in 🎮 free play, and see the famous failure case on the myth page (the next step) — where more training can't help.

This demo runs one pattern: Rings.
Why this one? it's complex enough to be interesting but well within this small network's capacity — so you watch it actually get there. Press Train and watch the right square sharpen into the left. (Every pattern is in free play.)
